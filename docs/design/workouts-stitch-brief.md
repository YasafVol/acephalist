# Workouts Stitch Brief

## Visual Thesis

Build the workouts section like a calm training field manual: monochrome, editorial, lightly technical, and more structured than the blog while still feeling native to Acephalist.

## Content Plan

- Hero: section title, current plan, single date, and direct entry into the active split.
- Support: short explanation of why the section exists and how exercise pages support the plan.
- Detail: day-by-day programming with grouped supersets and inline movement links.
- Final depth: exercise pages that hold description, images, key points, training cues, and video references.

## Interaction Thesis

- Use one restrained entrance reveal per page so the section feels deliberate rather than static.
- Keep link motion directional so the plan feels navigable and active.
- On the plan page, keep meta information steady while the day blocks scroll as the primary canvas.

## Translation Notes

- Preserve the existing Acephalist shell, typography, dark mode behavior, and border language.
- Introduce only one subtle training accent for chips and hover states.
- Avoid dashboard-card clutter; use long vertical rhythm, grouped rows, and strong labeling instead.
